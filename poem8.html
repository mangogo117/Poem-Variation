1. Sad

            is the man who is asked for a story
            and can't come up with one.


  2. His five-year-old son

            waits in his lap.
            Not the same story, Baba. A new one.
            The man rubs his chin, scratches his ear.


  3. In a room full of books

            in a world
            of stories, he can recall
            not one, and soon, he thinks, the boy
            will give up on his father.


  4. Already

            the man lives far ahead, he sees
            the day this boy will go. Don't go!
            Hear the alligator story! The angel story once more!
            You love the spider story. You laugh at the spider.
            Let me tell it!


  5. But the boy

            is packing his shirts, he is
            looking for his keys. Are you a god,
            the man screams, that I sit mute before you?
            Am I a god that I should never disappoint?


  6. But the boy

            is here. Please, Baba, a story?
            It is an emotional rather than logical equation,
            an earthly rather than heavenly one,
            which posits that a boy's supplications
            and a father's love add up to silence.


A Story

        Li-Young Lee
